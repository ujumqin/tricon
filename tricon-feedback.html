Providing Excellent Feedback

Coaching for an "Out-Caring" Culture

"At Tricon Residential, feedback is the engine that drives our Standards of Excellence."

Click Next below to begin your journey.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

  • Reframe feedback as a constructive coaching moment.
  • Identify the "What" and "When" of effective feedback.
  • Deliver feedback that upholds our "Out-Caring" philosophy.
  • Manage the impact of tone on team collaboration.

How Should We View Feedback?

Think of feedback like a Property Walkthrough.

When you walk a home and find an issue, you don't blame the building. You work to ensure it’s "Move-In Ready" for the next resident. Effective leaders:

  • Triage the situation (Is this a skill gap or a process hurdle?).
  • Diagnose the root cause with empathy.
  • Fix it through clear, actionable coaching.
"We aren't just checking boxes; we are ensuring every interaction is an Iconic Moment."

The Power of Tone

Small shifts in language move us from conflict to collaboration.

Avoid Absolute Words:
Words like Always and Never trigger defensiveness.
Instead use: "I noticed today..." or "Often..."

Avoid Blame-Heavy Words:
Words like Problem, Mistake, or Weakness.
Instead use: "Area for Growth" or "Iconic Moment Opportunity."

"Your tone should reflect the same care we provide our residents every day."

Knowledge Check: The Maintenance Pivot

Scenario: You notice a Tech was very fast on a repair but forgot to greet the resident or explain the fix.

“You’re too slow with the greetings. You never take the time to talk to residents and our NPS is suffering because of it.”

Is this an example of "Out-Caring" feedback?

a) True
b) False
Correct! This is accusatory and focuses on the metric (NPS) rather than the resident experience. A better approach focuses on the "Why": "I value your speed, but we missed a chance to build trust. How can we make time for that connection next time?"

Knowledge Check: Timing

When is the most effective time to provide feedback on a service interaction?

During the year-end performance review
As close to the actual event as possible
Only when a resident submits a formal complaint
Correct! Real-time coaching ensures the context is fresh and the learning is immediate.

The Iconic Feedback Loop: Summary

  • 🚀 Be Objective: Treat feedback like a property walkthrough—fix the issue, don't blame the person.
  • ⏱️ Be Timely: Deliver coaching while the 'Moment' is still fresh.
  • 🗣️ Watch Your Tone: Lead with empathy. Avoid 'Always' and 'Never'.
  • 🏠 Context Matters: Use Tricon Vantage and our Bill of Rights as your north star for coaching.
