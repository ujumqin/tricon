High-Level Design: Prioritization Framework

vILT Strategy & Workforce Efficiency Roadmap

1. Executive Summary & Business Case

The Goal: Empower team members to autonomously triage their workloads[cite: 1], ensuring "Iconic Moments" for residents are never sacrificed for non-critical administrative tasks.

The Opportunity: By introducing a standardized mental model for prioritization[cite: 1], employees can better align their daily efforts with organizational goals, leading to higher efficiency and improved professional well-being.

2. Target Audience & Performance Gap

Primary Audience: Individual contributors and frontline managers navigating high-volume workloads[cite: 1].

Desired State: Employees spend the majority of their time in Quadrant 2 (Important/Not Urgent)[cite: 1], focusing on long-term goals and prevention rather than reactive firefighting.

3. Learning Objectives (Bloom’s Taxonomy)

By the end of this vILT session, learners will be able to:

  • Define (Knowledge): Articulate the specific criteria for "Urgency" and "Importance" within the context of their role at Tricon[cite: 1].
  • Categorize (Analyze): Use the Eisenhower Matrix to sort current tasks based on business impact and time sensitivity[cite: 1].
  • Prioritize (Evaluation): Justify the allocation of time to specific tasks while identifying opportunities for delegation or elimination[cite: 1].
  • Strategize (Synthesis): Develop a habit-based plan to re-evaluate priorities at the start of each working day[cite: 1].

4. Instructional Strategy: The Eisenhower Framework

This 15-minute virtual session utilizes a Scaffolded Application strategy to ensure immediate transfer of learning to the workplace[cite: 1]:

  • Conceptual Grounding: Defining the two axes of the matrix—Importance and Urgency—through the lens of business reputation, financial impact[cite: 1], and project roadblocks.
  • Collaborative Triage: Interactive polls and chat-based activities where learners "speed-sort" common workplace scenarios [cite: 1], such as providing positive feedback vs. completing compliance training[cite: 1].
  • Real-World Application: Breakout room activities where learners apply the matrix to three actual tasks [cite: 1] from their current to-do lists to receive immediate peer and facilitator feedback.
  • Managerial Alignment: Emphasizing the "Quadrant 2" ideal [cite: 1] and the necessity of stakeholder alignment for effective delegation[cite: 1].

5. Measurement & Analytics Strategy

As a Learning Architect with an analytics focus, success is measured through the following funnel:

Level Metric Data Source
Level 1: Reaction Facilitator effectiveness rating and perceived utility of the Eisenhower Matrix tool. Post-session survey
Level 2: Learning Accuracy in "Speed-Sorting" exercises and polling results during the live session[cite: 1]. vILT Platform Analytics
Level 3: Behavior Self-reported increase in "Quadrant 2" time allocation 30 days post-training. Follow-up pulse survey / Manager 1-on-1s
Level 4: Results Improvement in project milestone completion rates and a reduction in "last-minute" emergency requests. Project Management Software
