High-Level Design: The Iconic Feedback Loop

Instructional Strategy & Internal Leadership Coaching

1. Executive Summary & Business Case

The Goal: Transition internal leadership from a "Transactional" corrective mindset to an "Out-Caring" coaching culture.

The Opportunity: By aligning internal feedback with the same "Standards of Excellence" provided to residents, Tricon can improve employee psychological safety, reduce turnover, and ensure consistent service delivery.

The Mission: Move beyond "checking boxes" to ensuring every internal interaction is a coaching moment that enables "Iconic Moments" in the field.

2. Target Audience & Performance Gap

Primary Audience: People Leaders, Maintenance Supervisors, and Operations Managers.

Desired State: Leaders utilize the "Property Walkthrough" metaphor to triage and diagnose performance gaps with empathy.

3. Learning Objectives (Bloom’s Taxonomy)

  • Reframe (Cognitive): Shift the perception of feedback from a "criticism" to a constructive coaching moment.
  • Identify (Analyze): Recognize the appropriate "What" and "When" for delivering effective feedback.
  • Apply (Synthesis): Deliver feedback that utilizes "Out-Caring" language while avoiding absolute (always/never) or blame-heavy (problem/mistake) terminology.
  • Evaluate (Evaluation): Manage and monitor the impact of tone on team collaboration and psychological safety.

4. Instructional Strategy: The "Property Walkthrough" Model

This module utilizes a Metaphor-Based Learning strategy to ground abstract leadership concepts in the daily reality of Tricon employees.

  • The Metaphor: Feedback is presented as a "Property Walkthrough"—a process of triaging, diagnosing, and fixing issues to ensure a home is "Move-In Ready," rather than finding fault with the "building" (the person).
  • Active Sorting: A drag-and-drop interaction requires learners to distinguish between "Out-Caring" coaching and "Transactional" correction.
  • Reflective Quiz Scenarios: Narrative-driven knowledge checks (e.g., The Maintenance Pivot) force learners to choose between metric-focused correction and behavior-focused coaching.

5. Measurement & Analytics Strategy

To prove the ROI of leadership coaching, we track data across the Kirkpatrick Model of Evaluation:

Level Metric Data Source
Level 1: Reaction Engagement with interactive elements and positive sentiment toward coaching tools. LMS SCORM Data
Level 2: Learning Assessment accuracy in identifying "Out-Caring" vs. "Transactional" statements. Quiz Pass Rates
Level 3: Behavior Improved employee engagement scores and "Manager Quality" ratings in annual surveys. Internal HR Surveys
Level 4: Results Correlation between high "Coaching Quality" and reduced frontline turnover. People Analytics / HRIS Data
