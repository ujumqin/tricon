High-Level Design: The Iconic Journey

Instructional Strategy & Implementation Roadmap

1. Executive Summary & Business Case

The Goal: Transition the culture from a property-first mindset to a people-first mindset, creating high-equity residents empowered to reach the 5-year Tricon Vantage milestone.

The Opportunity: By evolving from "Standard Service" to "Iconic Moments," Tricon can significantly increase Resident Lifetime Value (LTV) and reduce avoidable churn through strategic, empathy-driven engagement.

2. Target Audience & Performance Gap

Primary Audience: Resident Service Leads, Property Managers, and Field Maintenance Technicians.

Desired State: Staff utilize discretionary empowerment to solve the resident's human problem (stress, time, career) alongside the physical problem (repairs), navigating complex interpersonal dynamics with ease.

3. Learning Objectives (Bloom’s Taxonomy)

  • Identify (Cognitive): Recognize stress triggers in resident interactions, such as career milestones, the fatigue of moving, or family growth.
  • Evaluate (Analyze): Differentiate between a standard "Compliance-Based" response and a proactive "Iconic Moment" response.
  • Apply (Synthesis): Utilize the Tricon Vantage ecosystem, including Transfer Policies and Down Payment Grants, as a primary retention tool.

4. Instructional Strategy: The "Mirror of Action" Model

This simulation utilizes Conditional Branching Narrative logic. Unlike static assessments, this model uses a Persistence Engine to demonstrate the long-term impact of service decisions.

  • Relationship Equity Meter: A visual data point that tracks the cumulative impact of learner choices across the resident lifecycle.
  • Adaptive Narrative: The resident's dialogue and receptiveness change based on previous interactions, reinforcing the concept of "Service Debt"—the principle that poor initial service makes future retention exponentially harder.

5. Measurement & Analytics Strategy

To prove the ROI of this training, data will be tracked across the Kirkpatrick Model of Evaluation:

Level Metric Data Source
Level 1: Reaction Learner engagement & "Path Discovery" (Measuring replays to achieve 100% equity). LMS SCORM Completion Data
Level 2: Learning Difference between initial Relationship Equity score and final simulation score. Simulation Analytics Dashboard
Level 3: Behavior Qualitative change in "Resident Sentiment" scores following maintenance and service visits. Internal CRM / Resident Feedback
Level 4: Results Increased Retention Rate for residents reaching the 36-month and 60-month milestones. Internal CRM / Resident Data
